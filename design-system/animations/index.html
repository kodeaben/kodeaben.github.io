Animation language · v1 proposal

Motion that matches the marks.

Kodeaben is a code-built-to-last brand — but the cartoon-outline aesthetic invites a little play. The system below proposes a small, opinionated motion language: snappy by default, with one bouncy cousin reserved for moments of personality. Every animation here is live — hover, click, replay.

Motion principles

Five rules for keeping motion on-brand
01
Snap, don't ease
Default to --ease-snap — a slight overshoot mirrors the bold outlines. Reserve ease-bounce for personality moments.
02
Move shadows, not just shapes
Animating the offset shadow alongside transform is the brand's fingerprint. A button pressing flat is more on-brand than a button changing color.
03
Fast on input, generous on entry
Hover and press = 150ms. List entries and page loads = 400–600ms. Users tolerate slow when something is arriving; never when they clicked.
04
Stagger, don't synchronize
Sibling elements should arrive 60–80ms apart. Synchronous reveals feel mechanical; staggered reveals feel hand-built.
05
Respect prefers-reduced-motion
All transforms collapse to opacity-only. The cartoon weight already creates personality without movement.
